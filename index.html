Required Documents

Please prepare the following documents to accompany your application form:

  • Valid ID (National ID, Driver's License, Voter's ID, etc.)
  • Barangay Certificate of Residency
  • Certificate of Indigency (if applicable)
  • Medical Certificate (for medical assistance)
  • Death Certificate (for burial assistance)
  • Certificate of Registration/Enrollment (for educational assistance)
  • Senior Citizen ID (for senior citizen programs)
  • Recent 1x1 ID picture (2 copies for PWD and Solo Parent)

Application Process

Follow these steps to apply for assistance programs offered by the City Social Welfare and Development Office of Ligao.

Download and Complete Form

Download the appropriate form for your needed assistance. Fill it out completely and accurately.

Important Notes

  • Processing time may take 3-5 working days depending on the type of assistance.
  • Incomplete reqiurements will not be processed.
  • The office is open Monday to Friday, 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM.
  • Priority is given to senior citizens, persons with disabilities, and pregnant women.
  • For urgent medical assistance, please inform the front desk for expedited processing.

Frequently Asked Questions

The City Social Welfare and Development Office (CSWDO) is a government agency under the LGU mandated to provide programs and services for vulnerable groups such as children, women, PWDs, senior citizens, solo parents, and those in crisis situation.

Key Services include:
- AICS (Assistance to Individuals in Crisis Situations)
- Issuance of social case study reports
- ID and booklet issuance (senior, PWD, solo parent)
- Case management for abuse and domestic violence
- Shelter and rehabilitations programs
- Certificates of indigency and financial assesment

Any indigent resident of Ligao City experiencing a crisis such as:

- Medical emergencies
- Death in the family
- Lack of financial support for education
- Displacement due to fire or calamity
- Need for transportation, shelter, or basic assistance

You must provide:

- Medical abstract and final bill from the hospital
- Prescription or laboratory request
- Certificate of indigency
- Government-issued ID or certificate of residency
- Additional documents for anti-rabies cases (bite card, blotter, referral from CHO)

Submit:

- Death certificate
- Certificate of indigency
- ID or residency certificate
- Funeral contract
- Permit to transfer cadaver (if needed)

Through the Educational Assistance program, indigent students can get financial support.
Requirements include:

- Certificate of enrollment
- School ID
- Barangay certificate of indigency
- Parent/guardian ID or residency proof

CSWDO issues:

- Senior Citizen ID and booklet
- PWD ID
- Solo Parent ID
Each has its own checklist of requirements based on category and situation. Solo Parent ID applications require specific documents depending on your status (e.g., separated, widowed, rape victim, abandoned, etc.).

Prepare the following:

- Barangay Certificate of Indigency
- Certificate of Insolvency (from Assessor's Office)
- Sworn Affidavit of Tax Exemption (from Public Attorney’s Office)

A Social Case Study Report (SCSR) is a required document for legal or agency referrals.
Requirements:

- All documents from the related AICS category
- Referral letter or court order, if applicable
- An interview and home visit may be conducted before issuance

CSWDO provides case management for:

- Children in conflict with the law
- Victims of abuse or neglect
- Women experiencing domestic violence

Referral letters, blotter reports, or agency endorsements are required. The social worker will provide counseling, legal referrals, and shelter if needed.

The Core Shelter Assistance Program offers support for:

- Informal settlers affected by disasters
- Individuals with court-issued writs of demolition
- Those referred by concerned agencies or barangays

Vision

The CSWDO envisions equal access to social development assistance and opportunities for all clients and sectors, instilling community participation in a just and gender-fair society free from abuse, hunger, and poverty.

Mission

To effectively and efficiently carry out policies, programs, and services for and with the poor, marginalized, and vulnerable.
