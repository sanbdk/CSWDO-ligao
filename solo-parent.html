Required Documents for Assistance

Solo Parent ID Issuance

(Requirements vary depending on the reason for being a solo parent. Below is a general list covering all scenarios.)

  • Duly accomplished Solo Parent Application Form (from CSWDO)
  • Barangay Certificate of Residency and Parenting Status
  • Birth Certificate of the child/children (below 18 years old)
  • 2 pcs. 1x1 ID pictures
  • Additional Requirements Depending on Category:
    • Rape: Complaint affidavit, medical record, sworn affidavit of parental care
    • Death of spouse: Death certificate, marriage certificate, affidavit of no cohabitation
    • Detained spouse: Certificate of detention, marriage certificate, affidavit of sole custody
    • Physical/mental incapacity of spouse: Medical abstract, marriage certificate, affidavit of care
    • Legal/De facto separation: Court order or affidavits from disinterested parties, marriage certificate
    • Annulment/nullity: Court decree of annulment or nullity, marriage certificate
    • Abandonment: Affidavits from two disinterested persons, marriage certificate
    • OFW spouse/family member: OEC, passport stamps (12 months), affidavit of care
    • Unmarried parent: CENOMAR, affidavit from barangay official
    • Legal guardian/adoptive/foster parent: DSWD documents proving guardianship/adoption
    • Relative up to 4th degree of consanguinity: Death certificate or proof of abandonment
    • Pregnant solo parent: Medical proof of pregnancy, affidavit from barangay official

Application Process

  1. Get the Solo Parent Application Form from the CSWDO and determine which category applies to your case.
  2. Fill out the form completely and prepare all relevant supporting documents according to your situation.
  3. Submit the documents to the officer-of-the-day at CSWDO for initial evaluation and interview schedule.
  4. Proceed to the Social Worker or Program Focal for interview, case validation, and approval.
  5. Wait 3–5 working days for the processing of the ID and booklet.
  6. Claim the Solo Parent ID and Booklet at the CSWDO office. Sign the release logbook.
  7. Use the ID and Booklet to access benefits under the Solo Parents’ Welfare Act (RA 8972, amended by RA 11861).
